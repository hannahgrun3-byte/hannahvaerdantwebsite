The problem

You’ve built a business that works. The marketing grew up alongside it.

There’s a website, some email, a LinkedIn page, maybe a folder of half-finished plans. Each piece made sense when it was added. Nothing was ever designed to work together, and marketing has become the thing you think about at 9pm and move to next week.

Most growing businesses I meet don’t have a marketing problem. They have a clarity problem. The pieces exist. The structure doesn’t.

How I help

Part strategist, part builder. Inside your business rather than alongside it.

I work as a marketing partner. That means working out what actually matters (usually two or three things, rarely ten), putting them in order, and then doing the work. We decide what matters, then I build it. Strategy and implementation aren’t separate phases with a handover in between. They’re the same job, done by the same person, who is still here next month.

Before this, my career split across government, corporate leadership and creative studios, including years as an EA to government ministers and CEOs, and years working in creative studios on projects big and small. The government and CEO years taught me structure: how good leadership actually runs a business from the inside, and what quietly moves things forward versus what just looks like progress. The creative studios taught me the other half: what makes an audience actually connect with something, and why some projects land and others don’t.

About Hannah

I notice things. It’s a habit.

I’m Hannah. My career has moved between government, corporate leadership and creative studios: years as an EA to government ministers and CEOs, and years in creative studios on projects big and small. Different worlds, but the same habit ran through both. I notice things. I’m a curious person by nature, and I tend to absorb far more of a room, a business, a decision than I’m actually asked to.

That habit is the thread running through everything I do now. Still watching closely. Still curious about what’s actually going on underneath the surface of a business.

Now I do one job: helping a small number of growing service businesses simplify their marketing and build the systems that support it. We decide what matters, together. I build it.

I’m based just outside London and work with clients wherever they are.

Off the clock: a film camera, wherever the next trip is, and Barney, my red golden retriever and the best colleague I could ever ask for.

I use AI daily as a tool and never as a substitute for judgement. I work with a handful of clients properly rather than many at arm’s length. Both of those are on purpose.
